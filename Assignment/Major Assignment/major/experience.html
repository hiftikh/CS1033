I first was introduced to Adobe Photoshop CS3 when I was in grade 10. A bunch of my friends were into creating banners for Runescape for their online clan. I found it to be an interesting program to use. So I started to play around with Photoshop CS3 creating banners to just random pictures with filters. As time went on and I wanted to advance my skills, I looked onto Google and Youtube for tutorials on special effects. I used my skills of Photoshop for school projects to make them stand out from the rest of my peers. Then I went to creating posters for event for my high school’s Muslim Student Association.

At the end of high school, I knew the basics of Photoshop but still wanted to learn more. I took on more projects such as creating menus for restaurants to creating business logos. Adobe had a large arsenal of programs for creative use. I started to learn Adobe Illustrator to create vectors for logos and other things. This helped me since now I could use crisp vectors on Photoshop projects without blur and distortion.

I used the skills I had to apply for a job at Western Mustangs as a Graphic Designer. During this job, I learned how to use Adobe InDesign which helped design magazines and pamphlets. Currently I’m taking CS1033 at Western University to better enhance my skills. I’ve been exposed to a lot of programs while taking this course such as Adobe Dreamweaver, Adobe Flash, Adobe Encode, IMovie and more.

I plan on taking CS2033 to better enhance my knowledge in multimedia. Now I am currently learning how to code in HTML with CSS and Javascript.
